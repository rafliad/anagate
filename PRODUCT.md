# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Anagate's public landing page is for people evaluating or discovering agentic payment infrastructure on Stellar: future Stellar builders, AI-agent developers, ecosystem community members, security-minded founders, and technically fluent reviewers. They need to understand the product idea quickly, see why it matters, and trust that the mechanism is concrete without reading a full Statement of Work.

## Product Purpose

Anagate is a policy gateway for AI-agent payments on Stellar. It evaluates a payment intent before signing and submission, returning ALLOW, BLOCK, or REQUIRE_APPROVAL. The gateway is meant to keep agents from holding unrestricted wallet authority while still allowing automated payments to operate inside clear policy boundaries.

Success for the landing page means a visitor can explain the core flow in one sentence: an agent proposes a payment, Anagate checks policy, and only allowed requests reach the relayer for signing.

## Brand Personality

Anagate should feel precise, protective, and builder-native. The voice is confident but not inflated: it speaks in concrete verbs, explicit states, and verifiable flows. It should feel like a category-defining infrastructure product, not a grant pitch, generic AI-security page, or over-finished dashboard for a product that is still early.

## Anti-references

Do not make the public page feel like funding paperwork, a 30-day build announcement, or a grant application. Do not mention Instawards, grants, budget, sprint timing, or award language.

Avoid generic SaaS/security patterns: dark gradient hero blocks, endless metric strips, repeated numbered section eyebrows, identical icon-card grids, fake enterprise maturity, glassmorphism, and vague AI buzzwords. The page should not pretend the project is already a mature production platform.

## Design Principles

1. Make the gate visible. The Anagate mark should become a memorable visual idea: a gate between agent intent and signed execution.
2. Show the mechanism before the vision gets abstract. Payment intent, policy decision, relayer, and audit trail should be visible early.
3. Be honest about maturity without sounding unfinished. Present Anagate as a product concept and infrastructure direction, not a completed enterprise suite.
4. Write for builders and reviewers at once. Keep the story accessible, but preserve the exact control states and Stellar context.
5. Treat trust as a flow, not a claim. Use decision records, reasons, and transaction hashes as proof language.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive states. The page should work with keyboard navigation, visible focus states, responsive layouts, and reduced-motion preferences. Motion can help communicate the gateway flow, but content must remain readable and available with motion disabled.
